## FEE TRANSPARENCY COMMITMENTS

### What Fees Cover

Fees cover access to institutional-grade infrastructure:

- **Settlement Rails:** XRPL and Stellar networks with 3-5 second finality
- **Custody Framework:** Multisig architecture with 3-of-5 signing threshold and HSMs
- **Automation Engines:** 28 engines handling coupon calculations, escrow management, compliance checks, and settlement orchestration
- **Compliance Controls:** KYC/AML screening, sanctions monitoring, ongoing compliance surveillance
- **Evidence Infrastructure:** Dual-chain anchoring, SHA-256 hash chains, audit trails, real-time dashboards
- **Governance Framework:** 134 documented capabilities across 5 operational layers

### What Fees Do NOT Cover

- Investment returns or guaranteed yields
- Insurance against investment losses
- Guarantee of coupon payments
- Financial advisory services
- Legal or tax advice

## PROHIBITED FEE REPRESENTATIONS

The following statements about fees are **prohibited** and must never be made by any OPTKAS representative:

| Prohibited Statement | Why It's Prohibited |
|---------------------|-------------------|
| "You'll make it back quickly" | Links fees to implied returns |
| "Fees are negligible" | Minimizes the financial commitment |
| "Small compared to returns" | Implies guaranteed returns exceeding fees |
| "We can waive/discount fees" | Unauthorized fee modification |
| "Cheaper than [competitor]" | Prohibited competitor comparison |
| "Fees guarantee you access to returns" | Links fees to investment outcomes |

### Approved Fee Discussion Framework

When a prospect asks about fees, the approved response is:

> "The onboarding fee covers compliance screening, KYC/AML processing, wallet provisioning, and trustline configuration. Platform service fees cover the operation and maintenance of the settlement infrastructure, automation engines, and custody controls. All fee details are in the Fee Schedule and the Participation Agreement. I'd encourage you to review them before making any decision."
