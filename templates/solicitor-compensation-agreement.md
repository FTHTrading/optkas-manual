## RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Company operates an institutional-grade settlement, evidence, and automation infrastructure;

WHEREAS, the Company seeks qualified solicitors to present verified platform information to prospective accredited investors and facilitate the client onboarding process;

WHEREAS, the Solicitor has completed the OPTKAS Sales Academy certification program and has been awarded Platform Solicitor certification;

NOW, THEREFORE, the parties agree as follows:

---

## ARTICLE 1 — SOLICITOR STATUS AND SCOPE

**1.1 Independent Contractor.** The Solicitor is an independent contractor, not an employee, agent, partner, or joint venturer of the Company. The Solicitor has no authority to bind the Company or make commitments on its behalf.

**1.2 Legal Classification.** The Solicitor is classified as a Platform Solicitor. The Solicitor is NOT a financial advisor, investment advisor, broker-dealer, or registered representative.

**1.3 Authorized Activities.** The Solicitor is authorized to:

- Present verified facts about the OPTKAS platform using approved materials only
- Answer technical questions using information from the Proof Pack and approved resources
- Facilitate the eight-step client onboarding process
- Deliver Proof Pack documents (PP-01 through PP-08) to qualified prospects
- Direct questions beyond the Solicitor's authority to appropriate Company personnel

**1.4 Prohibited Activities.** The Solicitor shall NOT:

- Provide personalized investment advice or recommendations
- Make guarantees about investment returns or outcomes
- Use any of the twelve Forbidden Statements (F-01 through F-12)
- Modify, annotate, or paraphrase any legal document
- Waive, modify, or discount any fees
- Contact prospects during the mandatory 48-hour cooling-off period
- Solicit personal investments through the solicitor role
- Enter into undisclosed referral fee or side arrangements
- Represent themselves as an employee or agent of the Company

---

## ARTICLE 2 — CERTIFICATION REQUIREMENTS

**2.1 Initial Certification.** The Solicitor must complete and maintain Sales Academy Certification, which requires:

- Completion of all nine (9) Sales Academy lessons with passing quiz scores
- Completion of all audio narration modules (95% playback threshold)
- Passing the 50-question certification examination with a minimum score of 80%
- Signing all lesson attestations
- Completing the Claims Library review

**2.2 Certification Maintenance.** Certification must be maintained through:

- Annual recertification examination
- Completion of any supplemental training modules released by the Company
- Compliance with all conduct standards (zero violations)
- Verification Engine score maintenance

**2.3 Certification Suspension.** Certification may be immediately suspended for:

- Any single violation of the six disqualification triggers (see Article 5)
- Failure to pass recertification examination
- Material misrepresentation to prospects
- Non-compliance with documentation requirements

## ARTICLE 4 — DOCUMENTATION AND REPORTING REQUIREMENTS

**4.1 Interaction Logging.** The Solicitor must log every client interaction with:

- Date and time of interaction
- Participants present
- Topics discussed
- Materials shared (with version numbers)
- Questions asked by the prospect
- Compliance checkbox confirming all statements were within approved claims

**4.2 Proof Pack Delivery Logging.** For every Proof Pack delivery:

- What documents were shared
- With whom (full name)
- When (date, time)
- Via what channel (in-person, email, secure portal)
- Confirmation of receipt

**4.3 Audit Accessibility.** All logs must be maintained for a minimum of seven (7) years and must be available for audit upon request with 48 hours' notice.

## ARTICLE 5 — CONDUCT STANDARDS AND DISQUALIFICATION

**5.1 Immediate Disqualification Triggers.** Any single occurrence results in immediate certification suspension, investigation, and potential permanent disqualification:

| Trigger | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| DQ-1 | Using any Forbidden Statement (F-01 through F-12) |
| DQ-2 | Modifying or skipping steps in the eight-step onboarding process |
| DQ-3 | Contacting a prospect during the 48-hour cooling-off period (unless prospect initiates) |
| DQ-4 | Failing to provide the Solicitor Disclosure Form (PP-03) before agreement signing |
| DQ-5 | Undisclosed referral fees or side arrangements |
| DQ-6 | Personal investment solicitation through the solicitor role |

**5.2 Investigation Process.**

Upon identification of a potential violation:
1. Certification is immediately suspended (no client contact during suspension)
2. Compliance team conducts formal investigation within 10 business days
3. Solicitor is notified in writing of findings
4. Determination: Reinstatement, additional training, or permanent disqualification
5. Appeal process available within 15 business days of determination

**5.3 No Warning System.** There are no warnings for the six disqualification triggers. Any single occurrence triggers the full investigation process.

**6.2 Termination by Either Party.** Either party may terminate this Agreement with ________ days' written notice.
